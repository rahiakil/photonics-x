> I've been looking into ZBLAN optical fiber as it's one of the clearer cases for manufacturing in space. 
>
> ZBLAN is a heavy metal fluoride glass fiber. It offers the potential for lower optical loss than conventional silica fiber and transmits across a broader wavelength range into the mid-infrared. These traits could support longer transmission distances and new uses in sensing and communications.
>
> On Earth, producing high-quality ZBLAN has been difficult. Gravity induces convection in the molten glass during the drawing process leads to microscopic crystals and defects that scatter light and limit performance.
>
> In microgravity, these issues are reduced. The glass stays more homogeneous during drawing. Recent work by Flawless Photonics on the International Space Station produced over 11 kilometers of fiber in about a month.
>
> The returned samples are under analysis, and microscope comparisons show much cleaner microstructures than Earth-produced fiber.
>
> Challenges remain. ZBLAN is more brittle than silica and has a larger minimum bend radius, which affects handling. The technology is still in the demonstration phase but commercial production is being planned,and the plan is to manufacture it in space.
