### Electric Truckers (@ElectricSemi)
_Sat May 02 16:05:50 +0000 2026_ · ♥ 1 · ↻ 0 · 💬 1 · [open on X](https://x.com/ElectricSemi/status/2050607707963490731)

> The AI optics industry refers to the intersection of artificial intelligence and optics/photonics. It encompasses two main areas, though the term often highlights the high-growth tech infrastructure side in business and investment contexts.
> 1. AI-Powered Optical Systems in Healthcare & Diagnostics (Specialized/Niche)
> Companies apply AI to optical imaging hardware for faster, more accessible diagnostics.
>
> Key Example: AI Optics, Inc. develops portable retinal cameras (e.g., Sentinel Camera, FDA-cleared) with on-device AI to screen for diseases like diabetic retinopathy, AMD, and glaucoma at point-of-care (primary care, retail clinics) instead of specialist offices.
> Broader Applications: AI assists in eyecare (e.g., detecting glaucoma/dry eye), lens manufacturing, frame design, and personalized eyewear via The Vision Council and others.
>
> This segment improves access, speed, and accuracy in vision health but is smaller-scale compared to the tech side.
> 2. Photonics & Optical Components for AI Infrastructure (The Booming Market)
> This is the dominant and fastest-growing interpretation. It involves using light (photons) instead of electricity (electrons/copper) for data movement in AI data centers, where massive GPU clusters create bottlenecks in speed, power, and heat.
> Why it matters for AI:
>
> AI training/inference requires enormous bandwidth between thousands of GPUs.
> Traditional copper interconnects hit limits on distance, power consumption, and density.
> Optics/photonics enable higher speed, lower power, lower latency, and better scalability via fiber optics, lasers, silicon photonics, and co-packaged optics (CPO).
>
> Market Size & Growth:
>
> AI optics market (transceivers, lasers, switches, etc.) projected to reach ~$90 billion by 2030, with strong double-digit CAGR. AI cluster optics alone grew rapidly (e.g., $16.5B in 2025 estimates).
> Driven by hyperscalers (NVIDIA ecosystems, Google, etc.) and the shift to optical interconnects.
>
> Key Technologies:
>
> Optical transceivers and pluggables.
> Silicon photonics (integrating optics on chips).
> Co-packaged optics (CPO) and near-packaged optics.
> Optical circuit switches (OCS).
> Photonic chips for energy-efficient AI computing.
>
> Major Players:
>
> Broadcom, Cisco, Intel, Marvell.
> Lumentum, Coherent, Applied Optoelectronics (AAOI).
> Others in lasers, PICs (photonic integrated circuits), and manufacturing.
>
> This segment is often called the "picks and shovels" of the AI boom — essential infrastructure enabling larger, more efficient AI clusters.
> Overall Outlook
> The industry blends traditional optics (lenses, imaging) with cutting-edge photonics. The photonics-for-AI side is exploding due to data center demands, while AI-in-optics (design, diagnostics, manufacturing) is steadily advancing across sectors. Growth is fueled by AI scaling needs, 5G/6G, and Industry 4.0.
