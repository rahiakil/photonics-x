>

>

> Nvidia released this video of its photonics co-packaged optics (CPO) switch with Lambda.
>
> The AI race is not only about stronger GPUs, but about wasting far less power while those GPUs talk to each other.
>
> With co-packaged optics (CPO), NVIDIA is putting the light-based communication parts much closer to the main networking chip, instead of placing them as separate plug-in modules at the edge of the switch.
>
> From NVIDIA's official blog on this
>
> "co-packaged optics (CPO) connects directly to the token economy. Network power is overhead: it keeps GPUs connected but doesn't generate tokens. Network failures are also overhead: they turn provisioned GPU capacity into idle capacity. CPO addresses both by reducing network power draw and removing a large class of pluggable optical components from the fabric.
>
> A 128,000-GPU data center using traditional pluggable transceivers requires roughly 655,000 discrete transceiver modules across the switching fabric. Each one is a potential failure point. CPO removes that component class entirely.
>
> Agentic workloads change the pressure on the network. A traditional inference request is relatively self-contained. An agentic request can involve planning, retrieval, tool use, multiple model calls, and follow-up reasoning. More data moving across the cluster. More points where network latency or failure affects the outcome.
>
> Multi-agentic inference needs elastic and resilient data movement, so GPUs are not waiting for data, while maintaining tokens per second and fast time to first token."

>
> <p>But the densest cluster was the chipmakers: Marvell, which dropped 10% a day after jumping 10% on news it&#8217;s joining the S&amp;P 500, and the rest of what strategist Ben Emons has dubbed the &#8220;Parabolic 7,&#8221; after a chip index that ran up nearly 100% in a matter of weeks. </p>
>
> <p>Rather than fleeing equities altogether—treasuries barely moved—the market rotated into peanut butter and paint. Smucker jumped double digits; <a href="https://t.co/fV99xdkRMu" target="_blank">Home Depot</a> and <a href="https://t.co/WPktW7Fnk2" target="_blank">Sherwin-Williams</a> led. Real estate, staples, and utilities finished up: the classic ballast against tech froth.</p>
>
> <p>“You&#8217;re seeing money flow into consumer names that have been unwanted and unloved,” Richard Steinberg, senior global market strategist at Focus Partners Wealth, told the <em>WSJ</em>.</p>
>
> <p>The trigger wasn&#8217;t clear, but there are real reasons to want out of chips and diversified away from the AI trade. Wall Street has to make room for <a href="https://t.co/lgeBNG30O3" target="_blank">SpaceX</a>, set to be the largest IPO ever on Friday, with OpenAI and Anthropic—<a href="https://t.co/4q8VmRWjBl">both</a> <a href="https://t.co/liXJ7Lknfx">now confidentially filed</a>—close behind. Annex Wealth&#8217;s Brian Jacobsen called the tech run an Icarus trade with the wings melting: Alphabet&#8217;s rare capital raise was the first warning, SpaceX the “shiny new toys” pulling money out. Though even that story has a wrinkle: SpaceX is already oversubscribed, with multiple $10 billion orders in.</p>
>
> <p>Also this week, inflation data lands Wednesday and Thursday, and a strong May jobs report last week has already pushed expectations for rate cuts further out. Funds tend not to sit in their most crowded, highest-beta positions going into an inflation reading that could move the Fed&#8217;s path, which could be why some froth got cut off now.&nbsp;</p>
>
> <p>The selling may also be less about selling than an absence of buyers. Founder ETFs&#8217; Michael Monaghan described it as buyers stepping back rather than a rush for the exits, dropping the price faster than the volume would suggest.&nbsp;</p>
>
> <p>Oil went the other way. Even after President Donald Trump said the U.S. would have to respond to Iran&#8217;s downing of a U.S. Army Apache helicopter near the Strait of Hormuz, crude fell about 3% to roughly $88, as the energy secretary said traffic through the Strait was picking up meaningfully.&nbsp;</p>
>
> <p>Certainly investors are now wondering if this is the start of a longer “pop” or just a one-time correction. For them, the best information will come as SpaceX debuts, and public capital gets to decide if they believe in the AI story or not.</p>
